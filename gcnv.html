The Problem

Storage that grew quietly past its useful size.

Enterprise GCNV environments accumulate slack the way an attic accumulates boxes — one cautious overestimate at a time. Quarterly reviews don’t catch it. Generic FinOps tools alert on it without telling you what to do. And the storage team that could fix it has six other priorities this week.

📦

Over-provisioned by default

Volumes are sized for peak load that never comes. Pools are sized for volumes that have since shrunk. The bill compounds quietly, month after month.

⏳

Manual review is too slow

Reviewing a hundred pools by hand takes a week. By the time it’s done, the data’s out of date. So it doesn’t happen — and the slack accumulates.

⚠️

Shrinking is genuinely risky

A wrong resize crashes the workload. Storage teams default to leaving margin because they own the outage. Nobody owns the over-spend.

How It Works

Continuous analysis. Human-in-the-loop action.

Four steps, repeating daily. You stay in control of every change.

1

Connect

You grant our service account two read-only IAM roles on your GCP project. No credentials change hands. You can revoke access in one click, from your own console.

2

Analyze

Every morning we pull seven days of Cloud Monitoring data — capacity, throughput, latency — for every NetApp pool and volume. Aggregates to p50, p95, and peak.

3

Recommend

Our optimizer compares each resource against safety thresholds and produces a specific resize target with the exact dollar impact — computed against your tier pricing and any committed-use discounts.

4

Approve

Your team reviews each recommendation in the dashboard with full reasoning attached. You accept what you trust, reject what you don’t, and apply on your own schedule. We verify the change on the next run.

Right-Sizing

Per-pool and per-volume recommendations grounded in seven-day p95 utilization, with a peak guardrail that hard-blocks any shrink where the lookback peak exceeded 90%. One transient spike never produces an unsafe recommendation.

  • Pool-aware economics — we model NetApp’s actual billing surface, so the savings number on the dashboard is the number that shows up on your bill.
  • Tier-correct sizing — recommendations respect the minimum capacity of Standard, Premium, Extreme, and Flex tiers, and round to legal NetApp increments.
  • Discount-aware pricing — plug in your committed-use or negotiated rates once; every dollar figure on the dashboard reflects them.

Safety Controls

You decide what’s touchable. Resources labeled mission-critical are excluded from every recommendation, full stop — no exceptions, no overrides.

  • Three exclusion modes — GCP label, exact resource name, or regex pattern. Use whichever matches your existing tagging hygiene.
  • Read-only by design — we never write to your infrastructure. Every recommendation requires an explicit human decision to act on.
  • Per-tier guardrails — configurable thresholds for shrink, grow, target utilization, and peak guardrail, tunable to your risk appetite.

General

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are the savings numbers on the dashboard?

The dashboard computes savings against your tier’s list price, multiplied by any committed-use or negotiated discount you’ve configured. The number you see is the number that shows up on your monthly bill once the change is applied — we verify the resize on the next pipeline run before we count it as realized.

What if a volume gets busy after we recommend a shrink?

Our shrink threshold uses 7-day p95 utilization, not peak. We hard-block any shrink where the peak in the lookback window exceeded 90% — one transient backup window or load spike will never produce an unsafe recommendation. You can also tighten the guardrail or label specific volumes mission-critical for full exclusion.

Do you auto-apply changes?

No, by design. Every recommendation requires explicit human approval. We don’t have, and never ask for, write access to your NetApp infrastructure. Trust comes from the analysis first; auto-apply is the conversation we have years from now, not the foundation we build on.

How do you handle mission-critical workloads?

Three exclusion paths. Any GCP label of your choice (we default to ethersoft-critical=true), an exact resource-name allowlist, or a regex pattern — whichever fits your existing tagging hygiene. Excluded resources never appear as a recommendation, full stop.

How long does setup take?

Under ten minutes for most customers. You add your GCP project ID, run two gcloud add-iam-policy-binding commands (or apply our Terraform module), and your first recommendation set arrives within sixty seconds.

How does pricing work when we have multiple GCP projects?

Our pricing is 2% of total monthly realized savings across every project you connect — not per-project, not per-TB, not per-seat. Connect as many projects as you want; there’s no penalty for breadth.

Will this work alongside our existing FinOps tooling?

Yes. We’re a specialist layer focused on NetApp Volumes, not a competitor to your existing cost management stack. Most customers run us alongside Cloudability, Vantage, or their internal FinOps platform — we surface the storage-specific recommendations those tools don’t catch.

What about AWS FSx for NetApp and Azure NetApp Files?

On the roadmap. The rule engine ports cleanly; the connector work is real. We’re focused on GCNV first because that’s where the slack is largest and the install base is growing fastest. Talk to us if multi-cloud NetApp coverage is a near-term requirement.
